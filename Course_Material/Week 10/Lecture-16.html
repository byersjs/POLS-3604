Lecture 16 | Congress and the President

Plan for Today

  • Discuss the presidents role in the legislative arena

The President, Legislation, and the Constitution

  • As we have discussed, Congress was the primary mover of the US government and the legislative arena

  • The president was to serve in a secondary role

    • The courts in an even lesser role

  • The president was conceptualized as the person who would execute the laws passed by Congress

  • This role has changed over time

The Presidents Constituents

  • Elected every 4 years

    • House every 2 years

    • Senate every 6

  • Can only be elected to two terms

    • 22nd Amendment

  • Constituency Size

    • Approximately 330 million people (the entire country)

      • House approximately 750,000

      • Senate approximately 4.5 million (median)

  • These differences impact how presidents interact with the legislative process

The President and the Legislative Process

  • Presidents are charged with “faithfully executing the laws”

    • Implementing and administering laws passed by Congress

  • However, presidents are given legislative powers in the Constitution

    • Recommending laws / legislation

    • Veto power

    • Appointments

    • Negotiating Treaties

Presidential Influence in the Legislative Process

  • Prioritizing policy / legislative areas

    • By stating priorities, presidents engage in agenda setting

  • Vetos

    • Presidents can use the veto to block legislation they disagree with

  • Veto threat

    • Presidents can use the veto threat impacts how Congress approaches policy issues

Presidential Tools to Convince Congress

  • “Presidential power is the power to persuade” (Richard Neustadt)

    • Presidents cannot command, they are not kings, they must bargain

  • Presidents bargain in many ways

    • Electoral Mandates

      • Try to capitalize on momentum of winning an election by pushing issue areas that they ran on during their campaigns

        • The people have spoke and clearly support the presidents policy objectives

  • Going public

    • Bringing issues directly to the people

      • Use the presidents national constituency to pressure members of Congress

    • Public speeches, press conferences, SOTU, social media

  • Unilateral actions

    • Signing statements

      • A presidents interpretation of a piece of legislation

    • Executive orders

      • Instruction to executive agencies on how to proceed

Final Thoughts

  • Presidents have cultivated many tools to help them increase their influence in the legislative arena
